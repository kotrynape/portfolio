About New Zealand Mountain Huts and the Never-Ending Gaming Fever

"They say that winters used to be colder, but I don't remember being cold when I was still a little girl, stuffing handfuls of snow into my mouth. More than the cold, I remember the minute breaks during games – the snow, crunching from the cold, was there instead of water, for refreshment. After a few handfuls, breathing deeply and with red cheeks, we ran back to the hill to slide down or hide from our friends who were starting to attack us with snowballs again. Energy and heat flowed through our bodies – with that hot joy of games, we could thaw even the coldest winters."
