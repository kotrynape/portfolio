What She's up to Now

Kotryna is translating a series of over 30 articles she wrote during her travels in Spain (2019) and New Zealand (2024), originally published in a small local media outlet. Reaching around 1,000 readers each, the articles explore themes of solo travel, solitude, human connection, and the search for meaning through adventure.

To Meet a Lithuanian in New Zealand: How Small Our Big World Is

"In twelve days, you can warm up to a new job, visit a country, or make a new friend. I haven't tested it myself, but I believe even people like me can learn to cook a dish perfectly in twelve days. In that time, you can finish a book or two, recover from the flu (though if you haven't recovered in twelve days, consult your family doctor), fall in love, or come to terms with losing someone dear. And yes, in that time, you can also complete the Camino Primitivo route."

Back to School in New Zealand

"To be fair, my knowledge of New Zealand was (to say the least) limited when I first planned my trip. In fact, it wasn't until after I'd booked my flight that I learned the country comprises two main islands: the North and the South."

About New Zealand Mountain Huts and the Never-Ending Gaming Fever

"They say that winters used to be colder, but I don't remember being cold when I was still a little girl, stuffing handfuls of snow into my mouth. More than the cold, I remember the minute breaks during games – the snow, crunching from the cold, was there instead of water, for refreshment. After a few handfuls, breathing deeply and with red cheeks, we ran back to the hill to slide down or hide from our friends who were starting to attack us with snowballs again. Energy and heat flowed through our bodies – with that hot joy of games, we could thaw even the coldest winters."
